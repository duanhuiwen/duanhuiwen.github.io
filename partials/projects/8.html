Experiment with Youtube Content Delivery Network

2013.11 - 2013.12 | Espoo, Finland

Key words: CDN, Youtube

Youtube is one of most popular video service websites that serves user-generated and other videos15 . User with a Flash-enabled or HTML5-enabled browsers can watch those videos. Figure 8 illustrates a typical sequence of steps involved when a user watches a Youtube video. Basically, before being acquired and expanded by Google16, Youtube video delivery framework has 1 front-end data center in San Jose (US) and 7 video data centers (all of them are in US). The data centers operate as a content distribution network (CDN) in a way that the frontend web sersers will rewrite the host portion of the URL of the movie file in the response. As a result of that, for a specific movie, different users in different contexts will be redirected to different data centers. In case the the redirected data center does not have the requested file, Youtube CDN framework will utilize a proprietary protocol to allow that data center to retrieve and cache the content from other data centers. The CDN framework enhances the availability and performance of the Youtube service.


Project goals:

  • Examine the Youtube CDN from 4-5 different locations: university, home and three Amazon EC2 instances (Ireland, Singapore, California). For each geographical location, we identified the content servers IP address corresponding to a certain number of predifined Youtube links (20).
  • Upload videos from different locations, observe how a new content gets populated over the CDN

Tools and technology:

  • Pytomo for crawling the destination IP information.
  • Amazon EC2.
